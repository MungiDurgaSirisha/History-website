Andhra Pradesh-Culture & Art

BURRAKATHA

BurraKatha is a form of storytelling with the help of drumming instruments and chime and includes a group that comprises of minimum of three minstrels who are specifically trained in family tradition.

VEERANATYAM

Veeranatyam, which means the "Dance of the Brave", is a traditional form of dance, which belongs to Andhra Pradesh, a state located at the south eastern coastal areas of India. This dance form is associated with religious significance.

BHAMAKALPAM

Andhra Pradesh is known for its rich culture, drama and music all over the world. Bhamakalpam is both a theatre and a drama form of dance style which was created by Siddhendra Yogi in the 17th century for the holy use of Kuchipudi artists.

KALAMKARI

Out of the many beautiful forms of handicrafts, the Kalamkari in Andhra Pradesh has always been favorite with the art and craft lovers. The Kalamkari craft of Andhra Pradesh involves the art of printing and painting of fabrics.

KUCHIPUDI

Kuchipudi is the famous classical dance form of Andhra Pradesh. It has its origin in Kuchelapuri village or Kuchipudi village near Vijayawada. Dancers perform their graceful movements and rhythmic footwork skilfully on a metallic plate.
